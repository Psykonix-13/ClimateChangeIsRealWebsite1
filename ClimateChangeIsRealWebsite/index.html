Causes of Climate Change

Burning Fossil Fuels

The combustion of coal, oil, and gas for electricity, heat, and transportation is the largest contributor to greenhouse gas emissions. This releases massive amounts of carbon dioxide ($CO_2$), trapping heat in the atmosphere.

Deforestation

Forests act as vital carbon sinks, absorbing $CO_2$ from the atmosphere. When forests are cleared for agriculture, logging, or development, this stored carbon is released, intensifying the greenhouse effect.

Industrial Processes

Various industrial activities, including the production of cement, steel, and chemicals, release potent greenhouse gases like methane ($CH_4$), nitrous oxide ($N_2O$), and fluorinated gases. These gases have a much higher global warming potential than $CO_2$.

Agriculture

Agricultural practices contribute significantly to emissions. Livestock farming generates large quantities of methane, while the use of synthetic fertilizers releases nitrous oxide. Land management changes also play a role.

Waste Management

Landfills are major sources of methane emissions. As organic waste decomposes in anaerobic (oxygen-free) conditions, it produces methane, a powerful greenhouse gas. Improper waste disposal exacerbates this issue.

Overconsumption

Our modern consumption patterns drive increased production of goods and services, which often involve energy-intensive manufacturing, extensive resource extraction, and long supply chains, all leading to higher emissions.

Effects on Our Planet

Rising Global Temperatures

The planet's average surface temperature has risen significantly, leading to warmer oceans, melting ice, and heatwaves. This warming disrupts ecosystems and human systems worldwide.

Extreme Weather Events

Climate change intensifies extreme weather, resulting in more frequent and severe heatwaves, prolonged droughts, devastating floods, intense wildfires, and powerful storms, impacting communities globally.

Melting Glaciers and Ice Sheets

Polar ice caps, glaciers, and ice sheets are melting at an alarming rate, contributing directly to rising sea levels and threatening freshwater supplies for millions.

Sea Level Rise

Global sea levels are rising due to thermal expansion of warming oceans and melting ice. This threatens low-lying coastal areas, leading to increased flooding, erosion, and displacement of populations.

Ocean Acidification

The absorption of excess $CO_2$ by oceans leads to increased acidity, which severely impacts marine ecosystems. This acidification harms shell-forming organisms, coral reefs, and the entire marine food web.

Impact on Biodiversity

Changing climates force species to migrate or face extinction as their habitats transform. This leads to a significant loss of biodiversity, disrupting ecological balances and natural services.

Solutions for a Sustainable Galaxy

Transition to Renewable Energy

Accelerating the shift from fossil fuels to clean, renewable energy sources like solar, wind, hydro, and geothermal power is crucial. This reduces greenhouse gas emissions from electricity generation.

Learn More

Improve Energy Efficiency

Implementing energy-efficient technologies and practices in homes, businesses, and industries can significantly reduce overall energy consumption and demand, thereby lowering emissions.

Learn More

Sustainable Transportation

Promoting electric vehicles, investing in robust public transportation, and encouraging active transport like cycling and walking can drastically cut emissions from the transportation sector.

Learn More

Reforestation and Afforestation

Protecting existing forests, planting new trees (reforestation), and establishing forests on lands that were not previously forested (afforestation) are powerful ways to absorb $CO_2$ from the atmosphere.

Learn More

Sustainable Agriculture

Adopting sustainable farming practices, such as reduced tillage, improved manure management, and promoting plant-based diets, can significantly lower agricultural greenhouse gas emissions.

Learn More

Waste Reduction and Recycling

Minimizing waste generation, increasing recycling and composting rates, and improving waste-to-energy technologies help reduce methane emissions from landfills and conserve resources.
